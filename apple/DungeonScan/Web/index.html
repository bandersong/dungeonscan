How DungeonScan works

  1. Add your drawing — a photo of grid/graph paper, or a PNG, JPG or PDF exported from a drawing app (Procreate, Concepts, GoodNotes…).
  2. Line up the grid so the blue lines sit on your squares (Auto-detect does it for you).
  3. Read it — the app finds your walls, rooms, doors and stairs.
  4. Fix anything it got wrong by tapping — add or remove a wall, drop a door, draw a room, or stamp a symbol.
  5. Save a clean battle map (PNG/PDF), a tiled print-out, or a VTT file you load straight into Foundry or Roll20.

Grid look off? Zoom in with the ＋ at the corner of the picture, then drag the grid onto your squares — or tap − / + on Square size until the blue lines line up. If the photo was taken at an angle, tap Straighten corners first.

Everything runs on your Mac. Nothing is uploaded. The reading uses Apple's on-device Vision and a small model that lives inside the app.
